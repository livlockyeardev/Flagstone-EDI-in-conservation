EDI in Conservation:

It's Time for Change

In 2019, Conservation was ranked as the second least diverse sector out of 202.
Conservation 4 All is a charity on a mission to change this.
We offer resources and training to help organisations build inclusive diverse workplaces and create real change in the sector.

What is EDI?

  • Equality

    Everyone is treated fairly and has access to the same opportunities.

  • Diversity

    A workforce comprising of varied backgrounds, abilities and experiences.

  • Inclusion

    All individuals feel respected, valued and able to contribute fully.

What does it look like?

  • Fair Playing Field

    Access to experience and education for entry level career pathways.

  • Fair Hiring Process

    Interview and application process tailored to additional needs and backgrounds.

  • Comfortable Work Environment

    Spaces to work accomodate diverse needs and language is respectful.

Quick Guide to EDI implementation in Conservation

Power to the People

Encourage working groups for minorities in the organisation to inform policy change. Leaders and supervisors alone are unlikely to be aware of the needs of all groups in your community so it is essential that marginalised voices are heard.

Inclusive Recruitment Practices

Advertise roles widely, use accessible language, and remove unnecessary barriers that could favour certain socioeconomic groups over others. Women are 50% less likely to apply for jobs if they don't meet the full criteria. Ensure it is clear you encourage applications regardless.

Ongoing Education & Training

Provide regular workshops on equality, diversity, and inclusion to build awareness, challenge bias, and strengthen organisational culture. This should be regularly carried out to keep up to date with EDI best practice.

No free labour!

The conservation industry is notorious for requiring unpaid internships and volunteer hours to secure work. Ensure you don't offer this as an organisation nor ask for it as a job requirement.

Accessible Activities & Events

Consider inclusive social events that account for physical, cultural, and financial accessibility. For example, organise free and sober socials - as conservationists, anything outdoorsy is a safe bet!

Representation in Leadership

Promote diverse leadership and decision-making roles, ensuring representation reflects the communities served and inspires inclusive growth.

Funding the Next Generation

If you have the resources, consider investing in future conservationists by funding scholarships and paid experience opportunities in the UK.

Amplify Marginalised Voices

Have you come across an outstanding individual in conservation from a marginalised background? Give them a platform to share their story and inspire others.
